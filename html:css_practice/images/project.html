biography

FKA Twigs (born Tahliah Debrett Barnett 16.01.88) FKA Twigs is an English
singer/songwriter best known for her August 2014 debut studio album, 'LP1'.

Childhood: Tahliah Barnett was born in Gloucester, England, and raised by her
mother and stepfather. She attended secondary school at St. Edward's School in
Cheltenham, Gloucester, and started attending music related youth clubs at the age
of sixteen. The following year, Barnett moved to London, where she began a career as
a backing dancer for artists like Kylie Minogue, Taio Cruz and Ed Sheeran. She then
appeared in a music video for Jessie J in 2010, before serving with her again in 2011.

Career:In December 2012, Barentt (who had changed her name to FKA Twigs, with
FKA standing for 'Formerly Known As' and 'Twigs' being her old childhood nick-name)
self-released her musical debut. This came in the form of 'EP1', which debuted on
Bandcamp. For each song, she released a video on her YouTube channel. In August,
2013, Twigs released the music video for her first single on YouTube which was
picked up by The Guardian five days later and became part of their 6th August "New
Band of the Day" feature. In September of 2013, Twigs released her second EP,
named 'EP2'. On 6th August, 2014, FKA Twigs released her debut studio album,
'LP1'. It was nominated for the 2014 Mercury Prize.

LP1/2014



On LP1, Twigs expands her avant-pop landscape, yielding "boundaries-pushing" pop music.
Indeed, Entertainment Weekly felt that without Twigs' voice, it would be a work of avant-garde electronics.
It is seen for its "glitchy futuristic" take on R&B, casting the genre in "thrilling hyper-real hues".
